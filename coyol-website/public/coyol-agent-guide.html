Addressing the #1 Objection

"It's high maintenance to own a home in the jungle."

This is true — whether luxury or modest. The tropical environment is demanding: humidity, rain, insects, salt air. That's exactly why we build the way we do.

A home must be beautiful and comfortable, but it must also be sturdy, made with quality workmanship and materials, and built to withstand the elements with as little future cost or inconvenience as possible.

Our in-house capabilities exist precisely for this reason. We don't cut corners because we know what the jungle does to cheap construction.

What Happens After the Builder Moves On?

Every buyer has this concern. Most developers disappear after closing. We don't.

We educate buyers, manage expectations, and provide support and guidance long after they get the keys. Because we live here and operate restaurants here, we're not going anywhere.

It starts with building homes made to last — with materials, equipment, and techniques designed for this environment. Because most of our trades are in-house — including architects and electrical engineers — we're able to deliver on this promise and stand behind it.

What Buyers Want (That's Hard to Find)

Nosara's discovery and growth over the past few years have brought drawbacks. The very things that made it special — space, quiet, a relaxed pace of life — are harder to find.

  • Traffic and parking congestion
  • Petty crime
  • Ambient noise
  • Light pollution at night
  • Proliferation of Airbnbs and short-term rentals

These bring the very things people are seeking to escape back home in the city.

The Mar Azul Advantage

No Airbnbs. No short-term rentals.

This is a huge selling point. Your neighbors are owners, not rotating tourists.

The ultimate beauty of living in Mar Azul: close enough to Nosara for convenience and access to everything we love about it, but far enough for the tranquility, safety, and privacy we seek.

Market Context

Costa Rica's Luxury Markets

Peninsula Papagayo

Where Jeff Bezos landed. Home to Four Seasons, Rosewood, and Waldorf Astoria. Ultra-private gated peninsula with marina, golf, and private airport access. Estates from $10M to $50M+.

Why These Areas Command Top Dollar

  • Proximity to beach — walkable or short drive to sand
  • Ocean views — unobstructed Pacific vistas
  • Geography — ridgelines, points, and elevated terrain
  • Infrastructure — paved roads, water, power, fiber
  • Distance from airport — Liberia (LIR) direct flights from US/Canada
  • Direct road access — no river crossings or seasonal roads

Other Luxury Pockets

Playa Conchal — Westin resort community. White shell beach, golf course, gated luxury.

Tamarindo / Playa Grande — Most developed beach town in Guanacaste. Nightlife, dining, surf. Mix of price points.

Santa Teresa / Mal País — Surf luxury on the southern Nicoya Peninsula. Celebrity favorites.

Nosara — Blue Zone wellness culture, world-class surf, bohemian luxury. More authentic than resort destinations.

Escazú / San José — Central Valley urban luxury. Gated communities, country clubs, international schools. Where wealthy Ticos and expat executives live.

Dominical / Uvita — Southern Zone jungle meets ocean. Emerging market.

Testimonial

"I Could Have Lived Anywhere. I Chose Mar Azul."

When I sold my company, I could have lived anywhere. I chose Mar Azul.

Since my first visit, Nosara has always felt special, and I quickly fell in love with its natural beauty, kind people and good energy. It's been my most favourite place on earth and an integral part of my life for over 30 years, especially since this is also where, on my second visit all those years ago I met my lifelong best friend and fellow restaurateur Nosara-born Angelina Fraser-Peri, founder of the now iconic restaurants La Luna, Coyol and Esh Bakery and her husband, master planner, developer and builder Marion Peri.

Together, they brought their shared vision to life and bought a remote mountain-top farm perched above the beaches of Nosara and Ostional, where the air was a little fresher, the jungle lusher, the wildlife more abundant and the azure ocean views beyond spectacular. They called it Mar Azul. It seemed like a crazy thing to do at the time, but as they built the infrastructure necessary to serve the community they would one day welcome, it began to take shape, and of course, it all made sense. Build it, and they will come, indeed.

Twenty years in the making, their unique, elegant expression of curated modern luxury in a natural rustic setting, evidenced in everything they had already built and created, spoke to me, and I found a new home in Mar Azul. Next door to Coyol (if-you-know-you-know), sitting along two spectacular ocean-facing ridges, Mar Azul offers just 36 private homesites across 100 acres of pristine jungle. Space is the ultimate luxury.

The boutique residences of Mar Azul reflect my friend's commitment to elevated design, natural materials, connection to the land and a deep love & understanding of the region they also call home. A well-managed infrastructure is underpinned by an experienced team of local engineers, architects, landscapers and project managers. Clear rules on building standards help protect the community from overdevelopment and maintain the magical quality of life we are so grateful for.

While Nosara's Pacific coastline, beaches and neighbourhoods create a unique balance of wildness, wellness and warmth, its discovery and subsequent exciting growth over the past few years have brought some drawbacks. The desire for space, for those very things that made Nosara so special back then, a quieter, more relaxed pace of life, is harder to find.

At the end of the day, the ultimate beauty of living in Mar Azul is that it's close enough to Nosara for convenience and access to all the things I love about it, but also far enough for the tranquillity I seek. It combines the simplicity, peace, privacy, birdsong, natural beauty, safety and sense of community I have craved all my adult life, even as I hustled to make my dreams come true.

Ruth Tal
Founder, Fresh Restaurants
5× National Bestselling Vegan Cookbook Author
