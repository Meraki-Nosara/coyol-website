# Costa Rica Business Deductions

## Fully Deductible (100%)

### Payroll
- Gross salaries
- CCSS employer contribution (26.5%)
- Aguinaldo (13th month)
- Vacation pay
- Severance (cesantía)
- Workers' compensation (INS)

### Operating Expenses
- Rent (local comercial)
- Utilities (ICE electricity, AyA water, internet)
- Insurance (business, liability, equipment)
- Professional services (accounting, legal, consulting)
- Bank fees and card processing commissions
- Office supplies
- Cleaning and maintenance
- Security services

### Cost of Goods Sold
- Food and beverage inventory
- Packaging and supplies
- Freight and delivery costs

### Marketing
- Advertising
- Social media promotion
- Signage
- Printed materials

### Financial
- Interest on business loans
- Bank fees
- Credit card processing (Lafise, BCR commissions)

## Depreciation (Straight Line)

| Asset | Years | Annual % |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Buildings | 50 | 2% |
| Kitchen equipment | 10 | 10% |
| Furniture | 10 | 10% |
| Computers | 5 | 20% |
| Vehicles | 10 | 10% |
| Software | 5 | 20% |
| Leasehold improvements | Lease term | varies |

## Partially Deductible

### Meals & Entertainment
- **50%** of documented business meals
- Requires factura with client/purpose noted

### Vehicles
- Max depreciable value: ₡20,000,000
- Must prorate personal vs business use
- Keep mileage log for documentation

### Home Office (if applicable)
- Proportional to space used
- Utilities, internet, rent portion

## Not Deductible

- Personal expenses
- Fines, penalties, late fees
- Income tax payments
- Political contributions
- Expenses without factura electrónica
- Cash payments over ₡1,000,000 (must be banked)
- Donations exceeding 10% of net income

## Documentation Requirements

Every deduction needs:
1. **Factura electrónica** — valid Hacienda format
2. **Bank record** — payment proof
3. **Business purpose** — clear connection to operations

### Record Retention
Keep all records for **5 years** after fiscal year end.
